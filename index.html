Swimming Lessons

At Big Swims, we understand the importance of equipping children with essential swimming skills, which is why we offer private lessons tailored to their individual needs. Our dedicated instructors focus on building confidence and proficiency in the water, ensuring a rewarding and effective learning experience.

Each week includes five, 20-minute private sessions that run Monday-Friday. Your swimming lessons will be at the same time every day during your scheduled week. If you are registering more than one child, we do have multiple instructors allowing for more than one lesson at a time.

Parent Info

Teachers are assigned upon your arrival to your first lesson of the week. You will have the same teacher for the week unless we have a substitute for our instructor. You may have a different teacher for another week. We recommend practicing without goggles first to help your child feel confident in their breath work and swimming skills with water touching their face.

Please have your child use the restroom before you arrive at your lesson. This helps us use valuable lesson time on skills. However, we do have a bathroom if needed. There is a playground in the back of the yard. If you have children waiting for a lesson, they are welcome to play. Please keep an eye on your little ones for safety and noise control as our focus will be on kids in the pool.

As parents, it’s so hard to see our kids nervous or upset. Our teachers are loving, fun and understand that kids might cry intermittently throughout their lesson. They will continue with the lesson and work though skills unless it is excessive. We love one on one lessons! It’s so amazing to watch our kids grow in confidence and safety in such a short period of time. We can’t wait to see you all and get started.

Practice in the tub and shower! Teaching them to make “bubble cheek” or “chipmunk cheeks” while pouring a cup of water over their head or dipping their own head in the tub will help them significantly. Encourage them to get comfortable with water on their face through play.
